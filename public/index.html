About & Sources

This database compiles 400m race split times from major international competitions spanning 1968 to 2025. All data comes from official timing systems and published biomechanical analyses.

The pacing calculator uses split distribution percentages derived from this database, adjusted for performance level. Elite athletes distribute effort differently than developing athletes — at higher performance levels, the deceleration in the final 100m is proportionally smaller.

Key insight: The "differential" (200-400m time minus 0-200m time) is a key performance indicator. Elite men average 1.3–1.9s; elite women average 1.5–2.5s. Smaller differentials generally indicate better speed endurance and pacing discipline.

  • Brüggemann (1990) — Scientific research, Seoul 1988 Olympics
  • Reardon (2013) — Optimal pacing for 400m/800m, Am. J. Physics

Notes

Split times at the 0-100m mark include reaction time for some older entries. Where possible, reaction time has been separated in the source data. The "%" columns show each 100m segment as a proportion of total race time.

Youth/HS pacing models are extrapolated from established patterns — developing athletes typically decelerate more in the final 100m relative to elite runners.
